# Student-Performance-Visualization
This project involves a comprehensive analysis of student academic performance, aiming to uncover the socio-economic and behavioral drivers behind success. By processing a complex dataset of over 440,000 records, this dashboard provides educators and policy-makers with actionable insights into how internet access, parental involvement, and study habits influence exam outcomes.
The core objective was to move beyond simple score tracking and explore the "Why" behind performance gaps between Public and Private institutions.
🚀 Key Features & Insights
The Digital Divide Analysis: A deep dive into how internet access acts as a multiplier for academic success, correlating connectivity with both study volume and final grades.
Study Efficiency Mapping: Identification of the "Golden Ratio" of study hours—revealing that peak performance occurs at approximately 20–25 hours per week.
Socio-Economic Heatmap: Visualizing the intersection of family income, parental involvement, and resource access.
Demographic Performance Matrix: A faceted view of performance segmented by gender, school type (Public vs. Private), and motivation levels.
🛠️ Technical Stack & Skills
Data Cleaning & ETL: Used Power Query to handle null values, standardize categorical data (Internet Access, Resource Levels), and perform data type transformations.
Data Modeling: Implemented a Star Schema to ensure high-performance filtering across large datasets.
DAX (Data Analysis Expressions): * Created calculated measures for Total Exam Scores, Average Attendance, and Performance Ratios.
Used Time Intelligence functions and conditional formatting logic.
Advanced Visualizations: * Small Multiples: To compare gender and motivation across school types.
Dual-Axis Charts: To analyze the relationship between Attendance and Scores.
Donut Charts: For institutional distribution analysis (Public vs. Private).
📈 Dashboard Snapshots
Executive View: High-level KPIs showing total scores and student distribution.
Behavioral View: Correlation between hours studied and actual results.
Institutional View: Comparing resource allocation efficiency in Public vs. Private sectors.
💡 Key Findings
Public vs. Private: Public schools represent 69.57% of the student population, highlighting the scale at which intervention programs must be applied.
Parental Impact: Students with "High" parental involvement scores consistently outperform peers, regardless of income bracket.
The Motivation Factor: "High" motivation levels significantly bridge the gap for students with "Medium" or "Low" resource access.
